# CALPHAD-ML-codes-and-creep-data
Predicting the creep rupture life of Ni-based superalloys is challenging due to the complex interactions among material composition, microstructure, stress, and temperature. In this study, machine learning models were developed using 21 features, including alloy composition, operating conditions (stress, σ and temperature, T), and CALPHAD-derived microstructural descriptors (δ, Xγ′, Dγ). Among the evaluated models, Gradient Boosting Regression achieved the highest predictive accuracy, effectively modeling the complex, non-linear dependencies inherent in creep behavior. SHapley Additive exPlanations were utilized to quantify the contribution of each property, revealing stress, temperature, and key microstructural descriptors as the dominant features influencing creep life. Numerical Sensitivity Analysis, conducted by perturbing features by 10% of their desired range from SHAP analysis, further revealed that increasing alloying element concentrations, except Ti, is generally associated with improved creep life, whereas σ and T interactions significantly diminish it. Microstructural descriptors Xγ′ and δ are associated with increased creep resistance, while Dγ corresponds to reduced creep life. Overall, this work demonstrates an interpretable machine learning framework that integrates CALPHAD-derived microstructural descriptors with predictive analytics to enable alloy optimization and guide the design of high-temperature superalloys for applications such as turbine blades.
