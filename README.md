# CALPHAD-ML-CREEP
Predicting the creep rupture life of Ni-based superalloys is challenging due to the complex interactions among material composition, microstructure, stress, and temperature. In this study, machine learning models were developed using 21 features, including alloy composition, operating conditions (stress, σ and temperature, T), and CALPHAD-derived microstructural descriptors (δ, Xγ′, Dγ). Among the evaluated models, Gradient Boosting Regression achieved the highest predictive accuracy, effectively modeling the complex, non-linear dependencies inherent in creep behavior. SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) were utilized to quantify the contribution of each property, revealing stress, temperature, and key microstructural descriptors as the dominant features influencing creep life. Based on the SHAP-informed favorable ranges, Numerical Sensitivity Analysis was conducted by introducing 10% perturbations within the identified feature ranges to evaluate the model response and assess the sensitivity of predicted creep rupture life to feature variations. The analysis revealed that increasing the concentration of most alloying elements, with the exception of Ti, generally enhances the predicted creep rupture life, whereas the combined effects of stress (σ) and temperature (T) interactions lead to a significant reduction in creep performance. Microstructural descriptors Xγ′ and δ are associated with increased creep resistance, while Dγ corresponds to reduced creep life. Overall, this work demonstrates an interpretable machine learning framework that integrates CALPHAD-derived microstructural descriptors with predictive analytics to enable alloy optimization and guide the design of high-temperature superalloys for applications such as turbine blades.
